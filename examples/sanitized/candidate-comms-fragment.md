# Candidate Communication Fragment

Fictional scenario: senior product manager, B2B SaaS background, possible fit for an AI workflow product role.

## First Judgment

The candidate should not be pitched as a pure product execution hire. The stronger angle is workflow diagnosis plus productization ability. The main risk is whether the candidate has enough patience for enterprise implementation complexity.

## Opening Script

I saw that your past work is not just feature delivery. Several projects involved turning messy business processes into product modules. I have a role that may be closer to "workflow productization" than a standard product manager role, so I wanted to first check whether this direction is worth discussing with you.

## First Questions

- In your last two roles, were you mainly owning roadmap execution, or also defining workflow and adoption logic with business teams?
- Do you prefer mature product systems, or are you open to building process and standards from a less structured stage?
- What kind of business complexity would make a role attractive enough for you to consider a move?

## Stop Boundary

If the candidate clearly wants only a mature platform with stable process ownership, do not push. Move to long-term maintenance.
